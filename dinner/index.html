DINNER

by Jon Chan

Every week on Tuesday and on the last Sunday of every month, I cook a dinner party as a way of deepening new relationships, introducing my friends to each other, and honing my skills as an amateur chef.

Questions

Why are you doing this?

I really love how food people brings people together, and cooking is a great passion of mine. Hosting dinner parties is a great way to deepen relationships and meet new people.

How does this work?

First, RSVP with the dates you're available for this season, and pay $30 for the food and drink. Once you get a date confirmed and the payment is final, you just show up!

Why does it cost $30 up front?

It's to cover the cost of food and drink so you don't worry about bringing anything. I ask for money up front so people don't cancel last minute or at least cover their empty seat.

If I can't make it, can I get a refund?

I'll give you a refund if there's really no availability for the season, or if you let me know you can't make it before I send you a confirmed date. It's only fair that way.

What food do you usually make?

All kinds and I love to experiment. I'm a huge fan of taking a scientific approach to cooking. You can see my menus below for what I've made and am planning to make.

How did you learn to cook?

I'm a self-taught cook, but I really love ChefSteps and Heston Blumenthal as inspirations. I also have the help of many wonderful friends in the food industry.
