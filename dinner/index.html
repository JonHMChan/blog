DINNER CLUB

by Jon Chan

New York

Every week on Tuesday and on the last Sunday of every month, I cook a dinner party as a way of deepening new relationships, introducing my friends to each other, and honing my skills as an amateur chef.

Questions

Why are you doing this?

I really love how food brings people together, and cooking is a great passion of mine. Hosting dinner parties is a great way to deepen relationships and meet new people.

How does this work?

Just reach out to me! I'll ask you to pay $25 for groceries, all food and drink included. Once you get a date confirmed and the payment is final, you just show up!

Why does it cost $25 up front?

It's just to cover groceries, and so you don't worry about bringing anything. I ask for money up front so people don't cancel last minute or at least cover their empty seat.

If I can't make it, can I get a refund?

I'll give you a refund if there's really no availability for the season, or if you let me know you can't make it before I send you a confirmed date. It's only fair that way.

What food do you usually make?

All kinds and I love to experiment. I'm a huge fan of taking a scientific approach to cooking. You can see my menus below for what I've made and am planning to make.

How did you learn to cook?

I'm a self-taught cook, but I really love ChefSteps and Heston Blumenthal as inspirations. I also have the help of many wonderful friends in the food industry.

Menus

I always try and do something new every week, and I love bold, rich flavors. I tend to design menus about a month in advance, but things will change as ingredients come into season and inspiration strikes me.
